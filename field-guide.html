Reading a Flip, Line by Line

Keep this open next to the Desk. The formula, what every cost line actually is, and a real deal proving the buy price is the only number that matters.

The one rule
MAO = (ARV × 70%) − Rehab
1
ARV × 70% = your max all-in. The most you can have in the entire deal — purchase and rehab combined.
2
− rehab = your max purchase offer (the MAO). The number you actually offer the seller.

The 30% you leave behind isn't profit — it's profit plus every cost below. That's why you can't pay the full 70%.

You don't make money when you sell a flip. You make it when you buy it.

The ARV belongs to the market. The rehab belongs to the house. The price is the one thing you control — so the whole job is buying right, then holding your walk-away line. Get the seller to your number, or walk.

Five things that move the number
ARV from SOLD comps only. Garbage ARV in = garbage MAO out. Within 0.5 mi, last 6 months.
Rehab moves your offer dollar-for-dollar. Every $10k of rehab drops your MAO by $10k. Scope it before you lock a price.
The 30% covers costs first, profit second. Acquisition, holding, financing, selling all come out before you see a dime.
List it yourself, keep the listing side. ~2.5% of ARV most investors give away stays in your pocket.
Judge by the worst corner of the stress test. If it still profits at ARV −10% and rehab +30%, it's real.
